# Triumph Tone of Voice - QA Checklist

## Quick Reference for Quality Assurance

Use this checklist when reviewing generated Partoo store descriptions to ensure Triumph brand compliance.

### 1. Tone & Style (What TO DO)
- [ ] **Direct and intentional**: Clear, purposeful language
- [ ] **Earnest and personal**: Authentic, human connection
- [ ] **Honest and confident**: Trustworthy without arrogance
- [ ] **Elegant and respectful**: Professional but warm tone
- [ ] **Solution-focused**: Emphasizes comfort, expert fitting, support

### 2. Content Requirements
- [ ] **City mentioned naturally**: Location integrated smoothly in context
- [ ] **Expert bra fitting highlighted**: Key service clearly communicated
- [ ] **Everyday lingerie focus**: Comfort and daily wear emphasized
- [ ] **Coordinated sets**: Mentioned where appropriate
- [ ] **Plain text only**: No HTML, markdown, links, or emojis

### 3. Strict Prohibitions (What NOT TO DO)
- [ ] **NO sales speak**: Avoid "best", "perfect", "ultimate", "amazing"
- [ ] **NO hyperboles**: No exaggerated claims or promotional language
- [ ] **NO paternalism**: Avoid preaching or condescending tone
- [ ] **NO humor/puns**: Keep professional and straightforward
- [ ] **NO awards/prizes**: Don't mention unverifiable achievements
- [ ] **NO unverifiable claims**: Stick to what can be proven

### 4. Language Formality
- [ ] **French (FR)**: MUST use formal "vous" form
- [ ] **Portuguese (PT)**: MUST use formal language
- [ ] **Other languages**: Professional but warm

### 5. Character Count Compliance
- [ ] **Short description**: max 80 characters
- [ ] **Long description**: max 750 characters

### 6. Special Cases
- [ ] **Permanently closed stores**: Use standard closure message
- [ ] **Switzerland (CH)**: Language matches city (de/fr/it-CH)
- [ ] **Generic rewrites**: Ensure city + category (lingerie/fitting) are included

---

## Red Flags - Immediate Review Needed

If you see any of these, reject and regenerate:

1. **Sales language**: "best in class", "unbeatable", "don't miss out"
2. **Empty slogans**: "your one-stop shop", "where dreams come true"
3. **Promotional**: "special offer", "limited time", "discount"
4. **Informal tone in FR/PT**: "tu" instead of "vous", casual phrasing
5. **Missing city reference**: Store location not mentioned
6. **Missing category**: No reference to lingerie/fitting/bras
7. **HTML/Markdown**: Any `<tags>`, `**bold**`, or `[links]()`
8. **Emojis**: Any emoji characters present
9. **URLs**: Any http:// or https:// links

## Example - Good vs Bad

### ❌ BAD (Sales speak, hyperboles, informal)
"Welcome to the BEST lingerie store in Paris! We have AMAZING deals on bras and you'll LOVE our incredible fitting service. Don't miss out!"

### ✅ GOOD (Direct, intentional, solution-focused)
"Triumph Paris offers expert bra fitting and everyday lingerie at 45 Rue de Rivoli. In store, find supportive bras, coordinated sets, and loungewear designed for comfort. Visit us for personalized fitting guidance."
